What You Get

Everything Included in Your New CV

Every CV I write follows a deliberate structure designed to pass both human eyes and automated HR screening systems.

01

Clean, Distraction-Free Layout

A modern, readable design that puts your information first — no complicated text boxes, graphics, or multi-column layouts that confuse ATS (Applicant Tracking Systems) used by most companies to filter CVs before a human ever sees them.

  • Single-column primary structure for ATS compatibility
  • Consistent font sizing, spacing & alignment throughout
  • No tables, text boxes, or graphics that break parsers
  • Clean section headings that HR can scan in 6 seconds
02

Clear Hierarchy — Achievements First

Your CV is structured to lead with impact. Hiring managers spend an average of 7 seconds on a first pass — every section is ordered and written to grab attention and communicate value in those critical first moments.

  • Professional summary that opens with your strongest point
  • Achievements framed with action verbs & measurable results
  • Education section clearly structured with subjects or modules
  • Technical skills sectioned by category for quick reading
03

Delivered in Both Formats

You receive your finished CV in two industry-standard formats — so you are always ready to apply, whether a company wants an editable file or a locked, print-ready document.

  • Microsoft Word (.docx) — fully editable for future updates
  • PDF — print-ready, pixel-perfect, protected from reformatting
  • Files sent directly to you via WhatsApp or email
  • Clearly named files ready to attach to any application
.docx Editable PDF Print-Ready

Why layout matters more than you think: Many beautifully designed CVs with coloured columns, profile photos, progress bars, and infographics are invisible to ATS software — the automated systems used by most medium-to-large companies in South Africa. Your information gets scrambled or skipped entirely before any human reads it. Every CV I build is designed to pass those systems first, impress the recruiter second.
